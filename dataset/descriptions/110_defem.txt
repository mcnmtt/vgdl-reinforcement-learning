**Player role:**
The player controls a character that moves freely in all directions using standard movement controls. The character also has an invisible cannon that sometimes fires projectiles when the player moves.

**Entities:**
- **Floor tiles**: Hidden brown background tiles that cover the playing area
- **Invisible cannon**: An invisible object attached to the player that sometimes shoots projectiles
- **Player character**: The main character that the player controls directly, appearing as an axeman
- **Projectile**: A small axe that moves in random directions when fired from the cannon
- **Random moving enemy**: An enemy that moves randomly around the screen, pausing briefly between movements
- **Chasing enemy**: An enemy that actively pursues the player character, pausing briefly between movements  
- **Directional enemy**: An enemy that moves in straight lines in one direction
- **Random enemy spawner**: A hole that periodically creates random moving enemies, up to 10 total
- **Chase enemy spawner**: A different colored hole that periodically creates chasing enemies, up to 10 total
- **Rightward directional spawner**: A hole that creates enemies moving to the right, up to 5 total
- **Leftward directional spawner**: A hole that creates enemies moving to the left, up to 5 total
- **Upward directional spawner**: A hole that creates enemies moving upward, up to 5 total
- **Downward directional spawner**: A hole that creates enemies moving downward, up to 5 total

**Interactions:**
- When the player character moves over the cannon, there is a 50% chance a projectile will be fired
- When a projectile hits any enemy, the enemy is destroyed and the player gains 1 point
- When the player character touches any enemy, the player character is destroyed
- When the player character reaches the edge of the screen, they are pushed back onto the playfield
- When directional enemies reach the edge of the screen, they reverse direction and move the opposite way
- When random moving enemies reach the edge of the screen, they are pushed back onto the playfield
- When chasing enemies reach the edge of the screen, they are pushed back onto the playfield
- When projectiles hit enemies or reach the edge of the screen, the projectiles are destroyed

**Win condition:**
The player wins when all enemies on the screen have been destroyed.

**Lose condition:**
The player loses when their character is destroyed by touching an enemy.

**Objective:**
Survive enemy attacks while using your randomly-firing cannon to destroy all enemies that spawn from various holes around the level.
