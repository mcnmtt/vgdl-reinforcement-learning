**Player role:**
The player controls a character that can move in any direction using arrow keys or WASD. The character cannot pass through walls and will be pushed back when trying to move into them.

**Entities:**
- Floor tiles that form the walkable ground surface throughout the level
- Walls that block movement and form barriers in the level
- The player character that moves around the level
- Seeds that are placed on the ground and automatically spawn small flowers after a short delay, up to a maximum number of spawns
- Small flowers (stage 1) that automatically spawn medium flowers after a moderate delay, up to a maximum number of spawns - these are very small in size
- Medium flowers (stage 2) that automatically spawn large flowers after a longer delay, up to a maximum number of spawns - these are small in size
- Large flowers (stage 3) that automatically spawn fully grown flowers after an even longer delay, up to a maximum number of spawns - these are medium in size
- Fully grown flowers (stage 4) that remain stationary once created - these are large in size

**Interactions:**
- When the player touches a wall, the player is pushed back and cannot move through
- When the player touches a small flower (stage 1), the flower is destroyed and no points are awarded
- When the player touches a medium flower (stage 2), the flower is destroyed and the player gains 1 point
- When the player touches a large flower (stage 3), the flower is destroyed and the player gains 3 points  
- When the player touches a fully grown flower (stage 4), the flower is destroyed and the player gains 10 points
- When any flower stage touches another flower of a different stage, one of the flowers is destroyed

**Win condition:**
The player wins when the time limit is reached (after a long duration).

**Lose condition:**
The player loses if the player character is destroyed.

**Objective:**
Collect flowers of different growth stages to earn points, with larger/more mature flowers being worth more points. Survive until the time limit expires while managing the constantly growing flower population.
