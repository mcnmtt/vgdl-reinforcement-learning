Player role:
This is a two-player game where each player controls an avatar that can move around the game area and shoot cards. Player A controls a girl character and Player B controls a man character. Both avatars can move freely and shoot different types of cards when activated.

Entities:
- Floor tiles that form the game area background
- Player A's avatar: a girl character that can move and shoot cards
- Player B's avatar: a man character that can move and shoot cards
- Card deck for Player A containing: a 5 of spades, 10 of hearts, 2 of diamonds, and 3 of clubs, all displayed at half size
- Card deck for Player B containing: a 10 of spades, 2 of hearts, 3 of diamonds, and 5 of clubs, all displayed at half size
- Joker cards for both players that appear as blocks and can substitute for any regular card
- A central play area that starts with a neutral block and displays the currently active card
- Flickering projectiles that represent shot cards in different colors: blue blocks for spades, red blocks for hearts, yellow blocks for diamonds, green blocks for clubs, and gray blocks for jokers
- Hidden auxiliary markers used to track joker activation states
- A red genie character that moves randomly around the game area at moderate speed
- Walls that block movement and create boundaries

Interactions:
- When avatars, walls, or play area objects meet each other, the moving object is pushed back
- When Player A touches their deck cards, they transform to shoot the corresponding card type
- When Player B touches their deck cards, they transform to shoot the corresponding card type
- When players touch joker cards, hidden markers are spawned to track activation
- When Player A touches all four joker markers simultaneously, they can shoot joker cards
- When Player B touches all four joker markers simultaneously, they can shoot joker cards
- When joker cards meet their corresponding markers, the jokers revert to regular deck cards
- When avatars touch auxiliary markers, they are pushed back
- Any shot card can be played on the starting play area or on joker cards in the play area
- Playing the same card type that's already in play destroys the shot and penalizes the shooter by 1 point
- Playing cards in ascending order within the same suit gives the playing player 2 points
- Playing cards of the same rank in different suits gives the playing player 1 point
- Joker shots on joker play areas are destroyed and award 100 points to the shooting player
- When the genie touches any deck card, it transforms that card into a joker
- The genie is pushed back when it encounters walls, other deck cards, play areas, or avatars

Win condition:
The player with the highest score when time runs out wins the game.

Lose condition:
There is no explicit lose condition - the game ends when the time limit of 1500 time units is reached.

Objective:
Play cards strategically to score points by creating sequences and matching ranks while avoiding penalties, all while managing your deck as the genie randomly converts cards to jokers.
