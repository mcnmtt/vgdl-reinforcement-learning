Player role:
This is a two-player game. Each player controls a character that can move around and shoot projectiles. Player A controls a girl character, and Player B controls a man character. Both characters move at the same speed and can shoot flickering projectiles.

Entities:
- Green landscape elements that move leftward continuously, creating a scrolling background effect
- A brown train that moves rightward slowly and is invisible to players
- Moving rocky obstacles that travel rightward at medium speed
- Static mineral obstacles of two types - one type can only be destroyed by Player A, the other only by Player B
- Spawn points that occasionally create new moving obstacles
- Flickering projectiles shot by players - each player's shots have a unique type
- The two player characters - a girl (Player A) and a man (Player B)

Interactions:
- When either player touches the moving landscape elements or reaches the edge of the screen, they are destroyed
- When Player A touches moving obstacles or Player B's type of static obstacles, Player A is pushed backward
- When Player B touches moving obstacles or Player A's type of static obstacles, Player B is pushed backward  
- When the train or landscape elements reach the edge of the screen, they wrap around to the other side
- When either player touches the train, they are pulled along with it
- When moving obstacles are hit by Player A's shots, both the obstacle and shot disappear, and Player A gains 4 points
- When moving obstacles are hit by Player B's shots, both the obstacle and shot disappear, and Player B gains 4 points
- When Player B's type of static obstacles are hit by Player A's shots, both disappear and Player A gains 2 points
- When Player A's type of static obstacles are hit by Player B's shots, both disappear and Player B gains 2 points
- When Player A's shots hit the train, they transform into Player A's type of static obstacle
- When Player B's shots hit the train, they transform into Player B's type of static obstacle

Win condition:
Player A wins if Player B is eliminated. Player B wins if Player A is eliminated. If neither player is eliminated within the time limit, both players win.

Lose condition:
A player loses if their character is destroyed by touching landscape elements or falling off the screen.

Objective:
Survive on the moving train while shooting obstacles to score points and trying to eliminate the opposing player by creating hazardous obstacles they must avoid.
