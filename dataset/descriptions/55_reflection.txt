Player role:
This is a two-player game. Player 1 controls a cop character and Player 2 controls a sheriff character. Both players can rotate in place and shoot projectiles. The characters cannot move around the level, only turn to face different directions before firing.

Entities:
- Floor tiles that provide the basic ground surface (invisible)
- Black cliff areas that destroy anything that touches them
- A cop character (Player 1's avatar) that can rotate and shoot blue orb projectiles
- A sheriff character (Player 2's avatar) that can rotate and shoot different blue orb projectiles
- Mirror objects that reflect and redirect projectiles, oriented in four directions (up, down, left, right)
- Blue projectiles that travel in straight lines when fired - the cop shoots one type of orb and the sheriff shoots a different type
- Wall blocks that stop movement and projectiles
- When projectiles hit mirrors, they transform into special "attracted" versions that are drawn toward the mirror

Interactions:
- When either player character touches a wall, they are pushed back
- When a mirror touches a player character, the player bounces forward
- When a mirror touches a wall, the mirror is pushed back
- When a mirror touches a cliff, the mirror is destroyed
- When a player character touches a cliff, that player is destroyed
- When any projectile hits a wall or reaches the edge of the screen, it is destroyed
- When the cop's projectile hits a mirror, it transforms into a special version that is attracted to mirrors
- When the sheriff's projectile hits a mirror, it transforms into a different special version that is attracted to mirrors
- When any projectile hits a mirror, it becomes aligned and attracted to that mirror's orientation
- When the cop character is hit by the sheriff's attracted projectile, the cop is destroyed
- When the sheriff character is hit by the cop's attracted projectile, the sheriff is destroyed

Win condition:
Player 1 wins if the sheriff (Player 2's character) is destroyed. Player 2 wins if the cop (Player 1's character) is destroyed.

Lose condition:
A player loses if their character is destroyed by the opponent's projectile or by touching a cliff. If neither player is eliminated within the time limit, both players lose.

Objective:
Use mirrors strategically to redirect your projectiles and eliminate your opponent while avoiding being hit by their ricocheted shots or falling into the deadly cliff areas.
