Player role:
The player controls an avatar that can move around the game area and shoot missiles. The avatar can transform between two different forms - one that shoots missiles that create portal entrances, and another that shoots missiles that create portal exits. The avatar has a limited supply of weapon ammunition.

Entities:
- Floor tiles: Brown background surfaces that can be walked on
- Water: Blue liquid areas that are deadly to touch
- Weapon toggle stations: Two types of red staff objects that transform the avatar between its two forms - one changes the avatar to the form that creates portal entrances, the other changes it to the form that creates portal exits
- Weapon ammunition: Blue spaceship-shaped resources that provide the avatar with shooting capability
- Keys: Small red key objects that can be collected
- Locks: Door-like obstacles that block movement unless the player has collected a key
- Portal entrances: Light blue circular effects that teleport entities to the corresponding exit
- Portal exits: Brown explosion-like markers where entities emerge from portal entrances
- Goal areas: Green door objects that must be reached to complete the level
- Missiles: Small yellow projectiles that the avatar shoots - they move at medium speed and come in two types (one creates portal entrances when hitting walls, the other creates portal exits)
- Boulders: Potion-shaped objects that normally stay in place but can be pushed by the avatar, becoming mobile and following the avatar's movement direction
- Walls: Dirt-textured immovable barriers that block movement and stop mobile boulders

Interactions:
- When a mobile boulder hits a stationary boulder, it stops and becomes stationary again
- When two stationary boulders touch, they push each other back
- When the avatar touches a goal area, the goal disappears and points are scored
- When the avatar touches a portal exit, it bounces back
- When the avatar touches a portal entrance, it teleports to the corresponding exit and scores points
- When the avatar touches water, it is destroyed
- When the avatar touches weapon ammunition, it collects the resource
- When the avatar touches a key, it collects the key and the key disappears
- When the avatar touches a lock while carrying a key, the lock is destroyed
- When the avatar touches a lock without a key, it bounces back
- When any moving object touches a lock or wall, it bounces back
- When the first avatar form touches the first weapon toggle, it transforms into the second form
- When the second avatar form touches the second weapon toggle, it transforms back to the first form
- When the avatar touches a stationary boulder, the boulder becomes mobile and starts following the avatar's direction
- When the avatar touches a mobile boulder, the boulder follows the avatar's gaze direction
- When the avatar touches any boulder while another boulder is mobile, it bounces back
- When a mobile boulder hits a wall, key, lock, or weapon toggle, it stops and becomes stationary
- When a stationary boulder touches a wall, key, lock, or weapon toggle, all recent movements are undone
- When water touches a mobile boulder, both are destroyed
- When a mobile boulder touches a portal entrance, it teleports to the exit
- When missiles touch portal entrances, they teleport to the corresponding exit
- When missiles touch portal exits, they follow the exit's orientation
- When missiles reach the edge of the screen, they bounce back
- When the first missile type hits a wall, it creates a portal entrance and destroys the wall
- When the second missile type hits a wall, it creates a portal exit and destroys the wall
- When missiles hit goals or walls (in other contexts), the missiles are destroyed

Win condition:
The player wins when all goal areas have been reached and removed from the level.

Lose condition:
The player loses if the avatar is destroyed (such as by touching water).

Objective:
Navigate through the level using portals, keys, and boulder manipulation to reach all goal areas while avoiding deadly water hazards.
